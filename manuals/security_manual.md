## Introduction
This guide explains how to use the **Dispatch Management** application safely. It is written for non‑technical users who need to understand security‑related aspects of the system.

## Minimum Requirements
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Edge, or Firefox).
- Stable internet connection.

## Logging In
1. Open the application URL provided by your organization.
2. Enter your **Username** and **Password**.
3. Click **Login**.

> *If you do not have credentials, contact the Digital Transformation Department.*

## Security Features
- **Role‑Based Access** – you only see the screens and actions allowed for your role.
- **Session Timeout** – the system logs you out automatically after a period of inactivity.
- **Secure Connection** – all traffic uses HTTPS (look for the lock icon in the browser address bar).

> **Note:** Changing your password must be requested from the admin team (see Contact Information).

## Handling Errors
| Symptom | Possible Cause | Suggested Action |
|---|---|---|
| Access denied | Insufficient permissions | Verify your role; ask admin for proper access. |
| Session expires unexpectedly | Inactivity timeout | Log in again; avoid long idle periods. |
| Unexpected logout after login | Browser cookies blocked | Enable cookies for the site or try another browser. |
| Security warning from browser | Invalid certificate | Report to admin; do not ignore the warning. |

If the issue continues, see **Contact Information** below.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q:** How do I change my password?
**A:** Contact the Digital Transformation Department – they will reset it for you.

**Q:** Can I share my login details with a colleague?
**A:** **No.** Each person must have an individual account.

**Q:** What should I do if I suspect someone is misusing the system?
**A:** Report immediately to the Digital Transformation Department.

## Confidentiality & Ethics
- Do **not** share the application link, screenshots, or any data with unauthorized persons.
- Do **not** attempt to bypass security controls, hack, or scrape data.
- Store any printed reports in a secure location and dispose of them properly.
- Follow your organization’s data‑protection policies at all times.
